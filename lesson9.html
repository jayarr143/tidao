Pechay

Well pechay or petsay is the one of the easiest to grow vegetable in the Philippines. This kind of vegetable needs just 30 days before you can harvest it. This kind of vegetable is very easy to grow. You will just need to germinate it on a seed box and then transplant it to its designated location after. After that, you need to water it twice a day continue sly until 30 days of harvesting.The process of growing pechay is easy. You need to make your gardens plot. Using your gardens tool like grab hoe, cultivate and prepare the soil. After that germinate the pechay seeds inside the seed box or seed tray. When the pechay germinate and it has a few inches height and few leaves then you can now do transplanting. After that you can apply some fertilizer. Usually farmers buy fertilizer at the market, but we recommended to use organic fertilizer which it has no chemical content and it is good for health. After planting, applying fertilizer, watering, and caring for pechay, you can now harvest it in 30 – 45 days period. Harvesting pechay can be done in two ways. If you have more pechay planted, then you can get either two to four plant pechay and cook it. Or you can get older pechay leaves per plant and then harvest it. In that way the pechay will continue to grow and you can get more leaves.

After 90 to 120 days start harvesting young and fresh ampalaya. Harvesting ampalaya is easy. When you see the ampalaya with enough size and the skin is old enough, you can harvest it. String Beans

String beans or sitaw in tagalog is also best to plant in the Philippines. This kind of vegetable are long and growing. This is a vine type vegetable. To grow string beans, you need to directly plant it on the ground. As it is not applicable to indirect planting like growing it first on the seed box, it doesn’t works. You need to prepare your gardens plot. After that you need to get quality string beans seeds. Then using a stick or trowel make a hole on the ground, one inch deep and then put the seeds inside. Cover it with soil after. Then apply water and it will start to germinate. Then you need to put some wires or string so that it will climb on it. This will grow above the soil and it will hang on it. After three months you can now see some flowers and eventually turns to a young string beans. Harvesting string beans is very enjoyable. To harvest string beans cut it to its fruit base. The pods of string beans are long and cut it to the end of the fruit. Usually the base where it is located produce again more flowers so you need to be careful in cutting the string beans so that it will produce more fruits.

Squash

Squash or kalabasa is also one of the best vegetables you can grow in the Philippines. This vegetables produces bigger size squash and this will be great to grow in your backyard. There are varieties of squash which can give you small and also there are big. It depends upon on what kind of squash you have. Mean while in terms in growing squash, this will be also easy. You need to find a nice spot where you are going to grow squash. A place where there is a direct sunlight will be good. And a well drain kind of soil will be best, loam soil is recommended. You need to put one to two seeds per hole and then water it after. Put some fertilizer, organic fertilizer will be good. You need to apply water continuously while it continues to grow. After three to four months it will give you off flowers and turn to squash after. The squash can help you to have a better eyesight.

Malunggay

Malunggay or Moringa is also one of the best vegetables to grow. This has many vitamins and minerals content in which it can help you to live longer. This vegetables also is easy to grow. You can grow malunggay through seeds or by stem. If you are going to grow this from seeds, you need to get those dry and mature seeds. Find a spot where you are going to grow malunggay, a place where there is a direct sunlight will be best. And sand as its soil. Malunggay grow better in the sand. If you are going to grow this using the malunggay stem, you can also do that. You need to get a mature malunggay stem cuttings and directly plant it on the ground. Also try to make a little bit deep hole and then put some rocks around so that it will not be shaken. You need also to avoid watering it everyday as it prevents the malunggay to grow. After few months it will produce a great leaves you needed.

Like the three previous vegetables, you need also to grow it first on a seed box. After that start transplanting it to its, designated location. The process of growing pepper is very similar to those vegetables above. You just need to follow how. This vegetable is stronger. It don’t need much care as it grows well after you transplant it to its final location. After transplanting it, it will grows better and after four months, you can now harvest fresh peppers. It continues to bear peppers in the whole year. Harvesting peppers can be ripe or not ripe. Many cooks use green pepper and mix it to their dishes. The green pepper has a spicy taste but not as spicy compare to the ripe one. Some ripe peppers are used in many great dishes out there

Eggplant

Eggplant or talong in the Philippines is also one of the easiest vegetables to plant in the country. As if you try to notice or try to monitor in the market, there are many vendors of eggplant. This means that of there are many supply in the market, that vegetable is easy to grow. More farmers plant eggplant vegetables is because more people needs it. More people put it on a dishes that people cook. Planting eggplant is like also of the pechay and tomato procedure. Grow it first on a seed box, and then transplant it after. This kind of procedure is commonly used by the farmers because of its effectiveness. Do the following also; watering it everyday, apply fertilizer, and care for it continuously. After three to four months you can now start harvesting fresh eggplant from your garden. Harvesting eggplant is easy. You just need to cut it on its base and leave one to two inch long. The eggplant should have purple or violet in color as it indicates the eggplant is already ripe.

The seeds of lima bean is a little bit bigger. The seeds are smooth and soft. Also this vegetable vine is easy to grow so try to grow this plant. As compare to other vegetables this has a three to four months period of producing flowers and pods.

Okra

Okra or ladies finger is also one easy to grow vegetables in Ph. This has a stronger capability to grow in tropical country. Growing okra is easy, you will apply the direct planting and it will be easy to germinate. When the okra grows, it needs more sunlight to grow well. The more sunlight it gets, the better outcomes will show. After three to four months, you can harvest more okra. Harvest okra at a young stage because when okra is old enough it is not quite good to eat. To harvest okra, you need just to cut it to its base. Harvest young and fresh okra so that you can eat delicious vegetable.

Tomatoes

Well tomatoes or kamatis is also one of the easiest vegetable to plant in the Philippines. This kind of vegetables needs three to four months before you can harvest it. Planting tomatoes is just like pechay. You need to grow the seeds first on a seed box and after that transplant it to its designated location after.In planting tomatoes, you need to plant the young tomato seedlings about two to three inch deep with a plant spacing of six to 12 inches apart. After that you need to water it regularly especially when it is sunny. When you see that the soil is dry water it so that it will grow better. Aside from that you need also to apply fertilizer as usual. You need also to care for tomatoes because some pest or insect might damage your plants. Monitor every now and then so that you can remove them immediately. As times goes you will harvest tomatoes after three to four months. Tomatoes bear more fruits and it fast to ripen. You can harvest it when it is old enough and green in color. Try to avoid harvesting it when it is ripe if you are going to sell it in the market. But if for family purpose, you can harvest it when already ripe or orange in color.
